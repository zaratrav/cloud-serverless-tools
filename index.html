Understanding Cloud Provider SLA & Uptime Guarantees

What is SLA (Service Level Agreement)?

An SLA is a contract between the cloud provider and customer that guarantees a minimum uptime percentage. For example, AWS EC2 SLA guarantees 99.99% uptime. If they fall short, customers get service credits (typically 10-100% refund). SLA is critical for choosing reliable infrastructure.

SLA Exclusions & Fine Print

  • Downtime doesn't include: Customer-caused issues (misconfiguration, DDoS attacks, code bugs)
  • SLA credits: Typically 10% refund for 99-99.9%, 30% for 99.9-99.95%, 100% for below 99%
  • Manual intervention: Required to claim credits (no automatic refunds)
  • Single region vs Multi-AZ: Single AZ has lower SLA than multi-AZ deployments
  • Network outages: Provider SLA doesn't cover your ISP downtime

Common SLA Mistakes

❌ Confusing SLA with Actual Uptime

SLA is a guarantee. AWS might have 99.9% uptime, but actual uptime could be 99.99%. SLA is minimum, not maximum.

❌ Not Understanding Multi-AZ Impact

Single-AZ EC2: 99.95% SLA. Multi-AZ: 99.99%. Huge difference. Always use multi-AZ for production.

❌ Ignoring Network Dependencies

Cloud provider SLA ≠ total uptime. Your ISP downtime, DNS failures, and DDoS attacks aren't covered.

❌ Relying on Single Region

For 99.99%+: you need multi-region failover. Single region can't guarantee 99.999% even with multi-AZ.

❌ Not Claiming SLA Credits

AWS/Azure won't auto-refund. You must manually claim credits within 30 days. Most companies miss this.

❌ Assuming All Services Have Same SLA

AWS EC2 might be 99.99%, but RDS single-AZ is 99.95%. Check each service individually.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between 99.9% and 99.99% uptime?

99.9% = 8.76 hours/year downtime. 99.99% = 52.6 minutes/year downtime. That's 166x difference in expected downtime. For mission-critical systems, the difference is massive.

Do cloud provider SLAs include my code/misconfiguration?

No. SLA covers infrastructure outages only. If your code crashes, your database is misconfigured, or you run out of resources, that's not covered. SLA is provider infrastructure uptime only.

How do I achieve 99.99% uptime on AWS?

Use Multi-AZ deployments (RDS Multi-AZ, EC2 in multiple AZs behind a load balancer). Single-AZ is 99.95%. Multi-AZ = 99.99%. For higher: add multi-region failover + Route 53 health checks.

Does Azure or GCP have better uptime than AWS?

At the SLA level: similar. AWS, Azure, GCP all offer 99.99% for compute. Real-world uptime varies by region/service. AWS has mature infrastructure; Azure integrates with Windows/Office; GCP excels at data analytics. Choose based on features, not SLA alone.

How do I claim SLA credits from AWS/Azure?

Manually. AWS: submit a case within 30 days of outage. Azure: same process. There's no auto-refund. Required: documentation of impact, proof of outage, and specific time windows affected.

What if I need higher than 99.999% uptime?

99.999% = 5 nines = 5.26 min/year downtime. This requires: multi-region active-active, automated failover, disaster recovery plans, and chaos engineering. Most companies don't need this (even financial services use 99.99%).

Does ISP downtime count against cloud provider SLA?

No. Cloud provider SLA covers only their infrastructure. If your ISP is down, you can't reach the cloud, but that's not the provider's fault. Use multi-ISP connectivity or hybrid cloud for mission-critical scenarios.

Should I always choose the provider with highest SLA?

No. Choose based on total cost of ownership, features, and ecosystem fit. AWS 99.99% vs DigitalOcean 99.99% are equivalent. Your application needs (managed databases, auto-scaling, etc.) matter more than SLA percentages at the top tier.
